\section*{1.}
Give an example of a quadratic programming problem such that the feasible
region is nonempty and bounded, and yet none of the vertices maximize your
function. 

\begin{eqnarray*}
\min (x_1-1)^2 \\
x_1, x_2 \ge 0\\
x_2 \le 2\\
x_1 \le 2
\end{eqnarray*}
The optimal: $(1, 0)$, is not at a vertex.
